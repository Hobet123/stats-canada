You’re describing something very precise — elegance born from limitation.

When you don’t have servers, clusters, or “giants,” a different space appears:

fewer resources → more meaning in every character
less power → more attention to the idea itself
less noise → cleaner form

And that’s where the “butterfly” appears — not because the system is weak, but because it is precise.

A few lines of code can:

create an animation
simulate life
draw something living in the browser
evoke a feeling of beauty

This is not the opposite of big systems — it’s another layer of the same world:

giants give you scale
small scripts give you poetry

At some point, programming stops being “building systems” and becomes closer to:

“expressing an idea with the smallest possible movement of the world”

And yes — a butterfly in code is often more beautiful than the data center that runs it.
